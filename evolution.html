From MVP to Enterprise Platform

A Strategic Technical Journey

v1.0 (MVP)

Simple Information Aggregator

The MVP was a linear, proof-of-concept pipeline designed for rapid validation. Its primary goal was to connect a few sources and prove the core value of automated task creation.

Architecture:

Connectors RabbitMQ Processor Lark

Technology Stack:

K3s
RabbitMQ
EVOLVED

v2.0 (Platform)

Scalable Event-Driven Platform

Platform v2.0 is a complete architectural redesign, focused on scalability, robustness, and enabling advanced AI capabilities. It is built to be the enterprise-grade foundation for all future development.

Architecture:

A decoupled, event-driven system where microservices communicate asynchronously via a central event bus, allowing for independent scaling and development.

Technology Stack:

RKE2
Kafka
Redis
Strimzi
GitOps

Rationale for Change

The evolution from v1.0 to v2.0 was a strategic decision driven by MVP feedback and long-term vision. The initial pipeline, while effective for validation, lacked the resilience and flexibility required for an enterprise-grade product. The shift was necessary for:

Scalability

To handle massive data volumes from countless sources by replacing RabbitMQ with the distributed event streaming platform, Kafka.

Robustness

To build a fault-tolerant system where individual component failures are isolated and do not bring down the entire platform.

Future AI Capabilities

To create a flexible foundation for advanced, multi-agent AI workflows orchestrated with modern workflow engines and local LLMs.
